The History of the NHL in Canada

The National Hockey League (NHL) was founded on November 26, 1917, in Montreal, Canada, as a successor to the National Hockey Association (NHA), featuring only Canadian teams in its initial years. It quickly became the premier professional league, exclusively contending for the Stanley Cup by 1926. Despite expansion to the U.S. in 1924, Canada remains the heart of the NHL, with 7 of 32 teams currently based there. Key aspects of the NHL's history in Canada include: Founding Teams: The league began with four teams: Montreal Canadiens, Montreal Wanderers, Ottawa Senators, and Toronto Arenas. Expansion & Growth: While expanding to the U.S. in 1924, the NHL cemented hockey as Canada's primary winter sport through radio and television, creating national icons. Original Six Era (1942–1967): For 25 years, the league consisted of only six teams, two of which were Canadian: the Montreal Canadiens and Toronto Maple Leafs. Historical Dominance: The Montreal Canadiens hold the record for most Stanley Cup championships (24), followed by the Toronto Maple Leafs (13). Current Canadian Teams: Today, the league includes seven Canadian franchises: Montreal Canadiens, Toronto Maple Leafs, Ottawa Senators, Vancouver Canucks, Calgary Flames, Edmonton Oilers, and Winnipeg Jets.
